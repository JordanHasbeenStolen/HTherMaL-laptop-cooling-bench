Stability · AUC · Δ from baseline · Pareto · Boxplot
📦 Boxplot — Plateau temperature distribution (last 60s)
Box-and-whisker shows full distribution — median, Q1/Q3 (50% of data inside the box), min/max (whiskers). Narrow = stable. Wide = spikes. Median (orange line) = real working point, unaffected by outliers.
🌡 Δ-Heatmap vs Desk (°C over time)
What is Δ (delta)? Δ = condition temp minus desk baseline at each moment in time. Blue/purple = cooler than desk (good). Red/yellow = hotter than desk (bad). Shows not just the final result, but the entire warm-up path — who ran hotter early, who stayed below baseline.
〰 Volatility Heatmap (std °C within 6s windows)
Volatility = std deviation of temp within each 6-second window. Shows not the average, but the "trembling" of the curve. Dark = stable. Yellow = spikes (usually at cold start). More uniform row = calmer CPU thermal behaviour.
∫ AUC — Total thermal load over 5 minutes
AUC (Area Under Curve) = area under the temperature curve across the full test (°C × seconds). Accounts for both how hot and how long. Cold Start has a high AUC because it held a high plateau for a long time. Lower AUC = less total thermal stress on the CPU — matters for long-term hardware reliability.
◎ Pareto — Temperature vs Stability
Pareto front: X-axis = mean plateau temp (lower = better), Y-axis = σ (lower = more stable). Ideal = bottom-left corner. Highlighted points on the front are conditions where you cannot improve one metric without worsening the other.
📊 Plateau std — Stability on plateau (lower = steadier)
Standard deviation of temp in the last 60s of the test. near-radiator — the absolute stability leader. This is exactly what your eye was picking up on the heatmap.
